Apartment Development in Kiserian

Kiserian is a town situated in Kenya's Rift Valley Province, within Kajiado County. It is bordered by the towns of Ongata Rongai, Ngong, Enoomatasiani, and Kisamis. Located at the base of the Ngong Hills, Kiserian lies along Magadi Road, adjacent to the Kiserian Dam. The town is notable for its vibrant Maasai community, with nearby Maasai villages such as Olteyani and Olooseos. Kiserian offers several social amenities, including primary and secondary schools, as well as a few institutions of higher education. In the Maasai language, "Kiserian" translates to "a place of peace."

Proposed Nyahururu Residence

Nyahururu, formerly known as Thomson Falls, is a town in Kenya located northeast of Nakuru. The town's name originates from the Maasai word e-naiwurruwurr, which means waterfall and/or windy place or place of storms. Nyahururu is situated in Laikipia County, although it previously served as the administrative capital of Nyandarua District until the headquarters was moved to Ol Kalou when Nyandarua became a county. There have been calls to reverse this change. With an urban population of 36,450, Nyahururu remains a key economic hub for the region, maintaining strong economic ties with both Laikipia and Nyandarua counties.

Proposed Residential Development in Syokimau

Syokimau is a residential area located in the western part of Machakos County, Kenya, just south of Nairobi and near Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The area is named after the legendary Akamba medicine woman, Prophetess Syokimau. She famously foretold the arrival of white settlers in Kenya and the construction of the Mombasa to Kisumu railway line. In her prophecy, she envisioned people of a different color arriving from Kisuani—modern-day Mombasa—carrying "fire in their pockets," a reference later understood as white settlers with matchboxes and guns. She also described seeing a long snake stretching from the Indian Ocean to Lake Victoria, symbolizing the future railway. Administratively, the majority of Syokimau is part of the Syokimau/Mlolongo ward in Mavoko Constituency, Machakos County.
